# index about population
Wayne <- Wayne %>%
  mutate(
    #counting the share
    WhiteShare    = White / Pop2010 * 1000,
    BlackShare    = Black / Pop2010 * 1000,
    AsianShare    = Asian / Pop2010 * 1000,
    HispanicShare = Hispanic / Pop2010 * 1000,
    
    #counting the proportion
    WhiteProportion    = White / sum(White) * 1000,
    BlackProportion    = Black / sum(Black) * 1000,
    AsianProportion    = Asian / sum(Asian) * 1000,
    HispanicProportion = Hispanic / sum(Hispanic) * 1000,
    
    #counting Location Quotient
    White_LQ    = (White    / Pop2010) / (sum(White)    / sum(Pop2010)),
    Black_LQ    = (Black    / Pop2010) / (sum(Black)    / sum(Pop2010)),
    Asian_LQ    = (Asian    / Pop2010) / (sum(Asian)    / sum(Pop2010)),
    Hispanic_LQ = (Hispanic / Pop2010) / (sum(Hispanic) / sum(Pop2010))
  )
# data prepare for bar chart
Wayne_df <- Wayne %>%
    as.data.frame() %>%
    select(NAME, WhiteProportion, BlackProportion, AsianProportion, HispanicProportion,
        White_LQ, Black_LQ, Asian_LQ, Hispanic_LQ)

# proportion
prop_long <- Wayne_df %>%
    select(NAME, ends_with("Proportion")) %>%
    pivot_longer(cols = -NAME, names_to = "Group", values_to = "Value") %>%
    mutate(Group = sub("Proportion", "", Group), Metric = "Proportion")

# LQ
lq_long <- Wayne_df %>%
    select(NAME, ends_with("LQ")) %>%
    pivot_longer(cols = -NAME, names_to = "Group", values_to = "Value") %>%
    mutate(Group = sub("_LQ", "", Group), Metric = "LQ")

Wayne_long <- bind_rows(prop_long, lq_long)

Wayne_long$Metric <- factor(Wayne_long$Metric, levels = c("Proportion", "LQ"))

The Ethnic Group Population Proportion shows the percentage of different ethnic groups in Wayne Countys total population.
The map reveals that areas with a high proportion of white residents are primarily located in the western and southern suburbs of the county; the proportion of white residents is significantly lower in the downtown area.
Black residents are mainly concentrated in the northeastern downtown area and surrounding communities.
Asian residents are mainly distributed in scattered locations, near major transportation routes or commercial areas in the suburbs.
Hispanic residents are more evenly distributed, with the highest concentration in the south.

While proportion captures ethnic population structures, LQ captures the spatial importance of each tract for a racial group, which means it could identify areas with significant ethnic segregation.
Black residents are primarily located in the core of Detroit, with LQ scores reaching 2 or higher, which indicates a high geographical concentration of Black people in these areas and potentially suggesting ethnic community boundaries.
White residents have LQ scores significantly >1 in suburban areas and significantly <1 in urban areas, reflecting a clear trend of suburbanization.
Asian residents have LQ scores <1 in most areas, with only a few slightly >1, indicating that Asians are relatively dispersed within the county and are a later-immigrating group.
Hispanic residents have LQ scores >1 in some communities in the south, but the overall level is relatively low, reflecting that the Hispanic group is still in the early stages of a localized clustering and diffusion trend.

Overall, This analysis highlights pronounced ethnic spatial segregation in Wayne County.
African American residents are strongly concentrated in Detroits urban core, while White residents dominate suburban areas.Hispanic and Asian communities show emerging but still localized clustering patterns.
These patterns suggest long-standing socio-economic(e.g.industrial relocation, housing policies) and historical factors(e.g.historical segregation) shaping residential distribution.
